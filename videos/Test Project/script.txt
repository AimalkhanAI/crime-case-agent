
Title: The Zodiac Killer

Introduction

The Zodiac Killer was an unidentified serial killer who operated in Northern California in the late 1960s and early 1970s. The killer targeted young couples and individuals, often attacking in secluded areas. The Zodiac gained notoriety for taunting law enforcement and the public with cryptic letters and ciphers sent to newspapers, some of which remain unsolved. Despite numerous investigations and suspects, the Zodiac's identity has never been confirmed.

Timeline

- December 20, 1968: First confirmed attack, killing David Faraday and Betty Lou Jensen in Vallejo, CA.
- July 4, 1969: Second attack, killing Darlene Ferrin and injuring Mike Mageau in Vallejo, CA.
- September 27, 1969: Third attack, killing Bryan Hartnell and Cecelia Shepard at Lake Berryessa, CA.
- October 11, 1969: Fourth attack, killing Paul Stine in San Francisco, CA.
- July 26, 1970: Last confirmed letter sent by the Zodiac.
- 1974: Last possible communication attributed to the Zodiac.

Interesting Facts
- The Zodiac Killer sent over 20 letters to newspapers, including four cryptograms, only two of which have been solved.
- The case remains open, with multiple suspects investigated over the years, including Arthur Leigh Allen, though no definitive evidence linked him to the crimes.
